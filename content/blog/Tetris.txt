Gameplay
----------

At its core, Tetris is a simple yet addictive puzzle game that requires players to rotate and arrange falling blocks called Tetriminos to create a solid line without any gaps. The game is set in a rectangular playing field that is divided into two dimensions, with the blocks falling from the top of the screen to the bottom. As players clear lines, the game speed increases, and the blocks fall faster, creating a challenging and intense experience.

The gameplay is straightforward, but it requires strategy and quick thinking to clear as many lines as possible. Players can rotate the Tetriminos by pressing the arrow keys or use the down key to drop the block to the bottom of the screen. The game ends when the blocks reach the top of the screen, and players must clear as many lines as possible to achieve a high score.
